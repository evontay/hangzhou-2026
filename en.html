Hangzhou,
three nightsby the lake.

杭 州

Marco Polo called it the finest city in the world. Eight centuries on, Hangzhou still moves at the pace of a tea ceremony — willows trailing in West Lake, the scent of Longjing tea in the western hills, and the Grand Canal threading quietly past your hotel window.

West Lake at dawn — willows trailing in still water, a pagoda on the southern shore.
Day One · Arrival
20 May
Wednesday
A soft landing. Drop bags at the canal-side hotel, wander the old warehouse district on your doorstep, and let Hangzhou unfold gently.
11:10
Touchdown at HGH
杭州萧山国际机场
Land at Hangzhou Xiaoshan, terminal 4 (international arrivals). Clear immigration — Singapore passports enter visa-free. A Didi to Cheery Dragon Canal Hotel (杭州大运河非遗文化酒店) runs around 50 minutes; better than the metro with luggage.
Set up Alipay and a VPN before you fly. Cash is rarely accepted anywhere, and Google / WhatsApp won't work without a VPN.
14:00
Hotel check-in & lunch
杭州大运河非遗文化酒店
Cheery Dragon Canal Hotel (杭州大运河非遗文化酒店) sits along the Grand Canal — a UNESCO heritage waterway that flows from Beijing all the way to Hangzhou. After settling in, find lunch nearby.
浙江省杭州市拱墅区丽水路7号  ·  7 Lishui Road, Gongshu District, Hangzhou
15:30
Qiaoxi Historic Block
桥西历史街区
Right beside your hotel — Ming and Qing dynasty warehouses converted into shops, galleries and tea houses along the canal's western bank. The China Knife & Scissor Museum (中国刀剪剑博物馆), Umbrella Museum (中国伞博物馆) and Fan Museum (中国扇博物馆) are all here, all free, and unexpectedly charming.
19:00
Dinner near West Lake
武林夜市
A short Didi to Wulin or the Hubin Road area near West Lake. Get your first taste of Hangzhou cuisine — perhaps dongpo pork (东坡肉) or West Lake fish in vinegar sauce (西湖醋鱼) — and a first glimpse of the lake by night.
Day Two · West Lake
21 May
Thursday
The full West Lake circuit — causeways, pagodas, and an island painted onto the one-yuan note. Start before the crowds, end with lanterns on the water.
08:00
Bai Causeway at first light
白堤 · 断桥
Begin at Broken Bridge (断桥) — the start of Bai Causeway, named for the Tang dynasty poet Bai Juyi. Walk west through Solitary Hill Island (孤山), past Yue Fei Temple (岳王庙), then onto Su Causeway. Six kilometres of willows, lotus pads and ancient stone bridges. The morning mist is the photograph you came for.
11:30
Lunch lakeside
Try Green Tea Restaurant (绿茶) on the lake — affordable, packed with locals, signature Hangzhou flavours. Or Grandma's Home (外婆家) for the same in a slightly grander setting.
13:30
Three Pools Mirroring the Moon
三潭印月
Take the boat to the small island in the centre of the lake — the one printed on the back of every one-yuan note. Three stone pagodas rise from the water; on the night of the Mid-Autumn moon, candles are lit inside each one and the reflections multiply across the water.
Boat tickets ~¥70. The island makes the lake feel infinite.
16:00
Leifeng Pagoda at sunset
雷峰塔
Climb the pagoda on the lake's south shore for the panoramic view — West Lake, the city, and the Wuyue mountains beyond. The pagoda was rebuilt in 2002 with an elevator inside the ancient ruins, which is either a miracle or a sacrilege depending on your taste.
19:00
Hefang Old Street
河坊街
A short walk down from Leifeng — Hangzhou's restored old commercial street. Snacks, traditional medicine shops, and the smell of stinky tofu mingling with osmanthus. Dinner here is easy.
Optional: Impression West Lake show — book ahead
Meijiawu — terraces of Longjing, picked one leaf at a time.
Day Three · Tea & Temple
22 May
Friday
West into the hills — incense at one of China's oldest temples, then green tea fields straight from the basket of last month's harvest.
08:30
Lingyin Temple
灵隐寺
Founded in 328 AD — older than almost anything you've ever set foot inside. Set in a forested valley west of the city, with monks in saffron robes, smoke from a thousand sticks of incense, and the cliffs of Feilai Feng (飞来峰) carved with Buddhist figures over a thousand years old.
A Friday morning is much quieter than a weekend. Reserve via Lingyin's WeChat the day before.
12:00
Lunch in Longjing village
龙井村
Ten minutes from Lingyin. Eat at a tea farmer's home — a simple meal of tea-smoked chicken (茶叶熏鸡), river shrimp, and seasonal greens, with fresh Longjing tea poured throughout.
14:00
Meijiawu Tea Plantation
梅家坞茶园
Walk through neat green terraces of Longjing (龙井) Dragon Well tea — China's most famous green tea. May falls right after the spring harvest, when the leaves are at their finest. Visit a tea house, watch the pan-frying, and buy a tin to take home — the difference between this and supermarket green tea is the difference between coffee and instant.
16:30
Su Causeway at sundown
苏堤春晓
Built nine hundred years ago by the poet-governor Su Dongpo. End the day where it began — but on the opposite shore, watching the lake flush pink and gold.
20:00
Optional: Grand Canal night cruise
京杭大运河夜游 · 武林门
From Wulin Gate, near your hotel. The canal's banks are lit, the boats are slow, and the families along the water at night are doing exactly what their grandparents' grandparents did in this same place.
Day Four · Departure morning
23 May
Saturday
A final breakfast, a last walk along the canal, and onto the train.
07:30
Breakfast — xiaolongbao
小笼包早餐
Find a local xiaolongbao (小笼包) shop near the hotel. Soup dumplings done properly are a Jiangnan birthright.
09:30
Check out — Hangzhou East
杭州东站
Didi to Hangzhou East Railway Station, around thirty minutes. Aim to be at the station by 10:00 for the 10:30 train.

Suzhou,
among the gardens& canals.

蘇 州

For two and a half thousand years, Suzhou has been the city of poets, silk merchants and exiled scholars who built private gardens to imitate the universe. Cendre Hotel (欣得酒店（苏州虎丘店）) sits in the heart of Gusu — the old city — within walking distance of cobbled canals and the most beautiful gardens in the world.

Pingjiang Road at dusk — lanterns reflected in the canal that has flowed for seven hundred years.
Day One · Arrival
23 May
Saturday
Step off the train, drop bags, and walk into a 700-year-old neighbourhood. Pingjiang Road is on your doorstep.
12:30
Cendre Hotel (欣得酒店（苏州虎丘店）)
Taxi from Suzhou Station, ten minutes. Cendre Hotel (欣得酒店（苏州虎丘店）) sits at the heart of Gusu District, the historic centre. Drop bags, lunch nearby.
江苏省苏州市姑苏区和园路99号  ·  99 Heyuan Road, Gusu District, Suzhou
14:00
Pingjiang Road
平江路
A canal-side street unchanged in seven hundred years. Stone bridges, lattice windows, water lapping at the foundations of houses still lived in. Walk slowly. Try squirrel-shaped mandarin fish (松鼠桂鱼), the local signature, or sit in a teahouse with Biluochun (碧螺春) — Suzhou's famous green tea.
A small wooden boat ride down the canal (~¥80) is one of the loveliest things you can do in China.
18:00
Pingtan in a teahouse
评弹
A traditional Suzhou storytelling form — one or two performers, a pipa (琵琶), a sanxian (三弦), and centuries-old ballads sung in the soft Wu dialect. The hotel's front desk can point you to the closest current venue. An hour will change how you hear the city.
Day Two · Tiger Hill & Shantang
24 May
Sunday
The leaning pagoda, a boat ride home along the city's oldest waterway, and a garden lit by lanterns at night.
09:00
Tiger Hill
虎丘
A small hill outside the old city — burial place of King He Lu, founder of Suzhou, who supposedly lies beneath the pond at the foot of the hill alongside three thousand swords. Crowning the summit: Yunyan Pagoda (云岩寺塔), a thousand years old, leaning slightly north — the Leaning Tower of Pisa, but older and built of brick.
11:30
Boat to Shantang
山塘街
From Tiger Hill, take the canal boat back along Shantang Street (山塘街) — a 1,200-year-old waterway commissioned by the poet Bai Juyi when he was governor here. Older, slightly more local feel than Pingjiang. Lunch at a noodle shop along the way; Suzhou-style noodles (苏式汤面) are a quiet revelation — clear broth, hand-pulled noodles, a few pieces of belly pork or river fish.
14:00
Lingering Garden
留园
One of the four greatest gardens of China, UNESCO listed. Smaller and more refined than Humble Administrator's. Famous for its rockeries, calligraphy steles, and a 6.5-metre-tall Taihu stone (太湖石) — a piece of fossilised limestone shaped by water and prized as the soul of any classical garden.
19:00
Songhelou for dinner
松鹤楼
A two-hundred-and-fifty-year-old restaurant on Guanqian Street — the legendary inventor of squirrel-shaped mandarin fish. Order it once in your life, here.
20:30
Master of the Nets — by night
网师园 · 夜游
The smallest of the great gardens, opened at night with red lanterns lighting the courtyards and live performances of Kunqu opera (昆曲) and Pingtan (评弹) in the pavilions — moving from one tiny stage to the next as you walk through. Genuinely unforgettable.
Book ahead — Trip.com, 2-3 days in advance
A classical garden — bamboo, a moon gate, a Taihu stone (太湖石) in the pond.
Day Three · The great gardens
25 May
Monday
Weekend crowds gone, city back to itself. The big gardens, a museum by I.M. Pei, a labyrinth of stones — and a long, slow Suzhou banquet to close the trip.
08:30
Humble Administrator's Garden
拙政园
The garden you came to Suzhou for. The largest classical garden in China — built in 1509 by a retired official who, the saying goes, "humbly" administered the world's most extravagant retirement project. Five hundred years of poets have walked these paths. Be at the gate when it opens.
Critical: pre-book on Trip.com, 3–7 days ahead. Daily cap.
11:30
Suzhou Museum
苏州博物馆
Designed by I.M. Pei (贝聿铭), born in Suzhou — his last great commission, completed in 2006. White walls, dark roofs, a stone garden you'll want to photograph from every angle. The collection is small and exquisite: Song dynasty calligraphy, Ming porcelain, Buddhist relics.
Free, but reservation required — book 7 days ahead
14:30
Lion Grove Garden
狮子林
Five minutes' walk from the museum. UNESCO listed, but completely different in spirit — a maze of rockeries that look like crouching lions, with caves and tunnels you can walk through. Built in 1342 by Buddhist monks. Children adore it; adults too.
19:00
Wumen Renjia — a Suzhou banquet
吴门人家
A traditional Suzhou banquet in a courtyard restaurant near Pingjiang. Squirrel fish (松鼠桂鱼), Suzhou-style braised pork (苏式红烧肉), osmanthus glutinous rice (桂花糯米饭), biluochun-poached prawns (碧螺虾仁). Slow, ceremonial, exactly the right way to end a trip.
21:00
A last walk along the canals
Pingjiang Road after the day-trippers have left. The lanterns reflecting in still water, locals on stoops, somewhere a pipa playing through an open window.
Day Four · Homeward
26 May
Tuesday
An early train, the airport, the long flight home. Hold on to a tin of Longjing for the journey.
09:00
Check out · Suzhou Station
苏州站
Taxi to Suzhou Station for the 10:00 G-class to Hangzhou East. Built-in buffer for traffic.
11:30
Hangzhou East — onwards to HGH
杭州东站 → 萧山国际机场
Off the train and straight to Hangzhou Xiaoshan airport. Metro Line 1 then 7, or Didi (faster, ~CNY 100). At the airport for 13:00 — comfortable buffer before the 15:55 Cathay Pacific flight to Hong Kong, then home to Singapore via CX715.
00:45
Singapore
新加坡 · 樟宜机场
Land at Changi T4, in the small hours of May 27. A week of mist, gardens, and tea.
